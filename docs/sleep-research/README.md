## Key Research Sources

### Primary Studies Referenced
1. **Gradisar et al. (2016)** - Pediatrics
   - Randomized controlled trial of sleep training
   - Measured cortisol, attachment, outcomes
   - Found no adverse effects

2. **Mindell et al. (2006)** - American Academy of Sleep Medicine
   - Review of 52 sleep training studies
   - Concluded behavioral interventions are effective

3. **Hiscock et al. (2007, 2012)** - Multiple publications
   - Sleep intervention trials with long-term follow-up
   - 5-year outcomes showed no harm

4. **Tham et al. (2017)** - Nature and Science of Sleep
   - Infant sleep and cognition/growth review
   - Positive associations between sleep and development

5. **Price et al. (2012)** - Pediatrics
   - 5-year follow-up of sleep-trained children
   - No differences in attachment or behavior

### Official Guidelines
- American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP) Safe Sleep Guidelines 2022
- NHS Guidance on Baby Sleep
- Lullaby Trust Safer Sleep Advice
- American Academy of Sleep Medicine (AASM) Recommendations
- National Sleep Foundation Guidelines

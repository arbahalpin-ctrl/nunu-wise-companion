## Sleep Needs by Age

### Newborn (0-3 months)
- **Total:** 14-17 hours
- **Wake windows:** 45-90 minutes
- **Naps:** Many short naps
- **Night:** Frequent waking normal
- **Key:** Survival mode, no training

### 4-6 Months
- **Total:** 12-16 hours
- **Wake windows:** 1.5-2.5 hours
- **Naps:** 3-4 naps, 2-4 hours total
- **Night:** 10-12 hours (with waking)
- **Key:** Can begin gentle sleep work

### 6-9 Months
- **Total:** 12-15 hours
- **Wake windows:** 2-3.5 hours
- **Naps:** 2-3 naps, 2-3.5 hours
- **Night:** 10-12 hours possible
- **Key:** Optimal sleep training window

### 9-12 Months
- **Total:** 12-14 hours
- **Wake windows:** 3-4 hours
- **Naps:** 2 naps, 2-3 hours
- **Night:** 10-12 hours
- **Key:** Can sleep through if trained

### 12-18 Months
- **Total:** 11-14 hours
- **Wake windows:** 3.5-5 hours
- **Naps:** 1-2 naps (transition period)
- **Night:** 10-12 hours
- **Key:** 2→1 nap transition

### 18-24 Months
- **Total:** 11-14 hours
- **Wake windows:** 5-6 hours
- **Naps:** 1 nap (1.5-3 hours)
- **Night:** 10-12 hours
- **Key:** Boundary testing begins

## Bedtime Routine Template

### Newborn (5-10 min)
1. Diaper change
2. Swaddle/sleep sack
3. Feed
4. Quick cuddle
5. Put down

### 4-12 Months (15-20 min)
1. Bath (2-3x/week)
2. Diaper & pajamas
3. Feed (not to sleep)
4. Book or song
5. Cuddles in dim room
6. Put down drowsy

### Toddler (20-30 min)
1. Bath
2. Teeth brushing
3. Pajamas
4. 2-3 books
5. Songs/quiet talk
6. Cuddles
7. Goodnight ritual
8. Leave room

## Sleep Regression Quick Guide

### 4-Month Regression
**What's happening:** Sleep cycles maturing
**Duration:** 2-6 weeks
**What helps:** Introduce independent sleep, stay consistent
**Note:** This is actually a progression!

### 8-9 Month Regression
**What's happening:** Separation anxiety, mobility, object permanence
**Duration:** 2-4 weeks
**What helps:** Extra reassurance, consistent boundaries
**Note:** May coincide with crawling/standing

### 12-Month Regression
**What's happening:** Walking, language explosion
**Duration:** 2-4 weeks
**What helps:** Don't drop nap yet! Stay consistent
**Note:** NOT time to transition to one nap

### 18-Month Regression
**What's happening:** Independence, language, anxiety
**Duration:** 2-6 weeks
**What helps:** Firm boundaries with extra love
**Note:** Common time for toddler sleep struggles

## Safe Sleep Checklist (ABCs)

### A - Alone
- [ ] Baby sleeps in own sleep space
- [ ] No bed-sharing
- [ ] No loose bedding, pillows, toys
- [ ] No bumpers

### B - Back
- [ ] Always on back for sleep
- [ ] Until baby can roll both ways independently
- [ ] Then they can find own position

### C - Crib
- [ ] Firm, flat mattress
- [ ] Fitted sheet only
- [ ] Meets safety standards
- [ ] No incline

### Plus:
- [ ] Room-share for 6+ months
- [ ] No smoke exposure
- [ ] Room temp 16-20°C / 60-68°F
- [ ] Breastfeeding if possible
- [ ] Consider pacifier

## Troubleshooting Quick Fixes

| Problem | Quick Fixes |
|---------|-------------|
| **Won't fall asleep** | Check wake window, darken room, routine |
| **Short naps** | Extend wake window, try crib hour |
| **Early waking** | Later bedtime, blackout curtains |
| **Night waking** | Independent sleep skills, rule out hunger |
| **Bedtime battles** | Earlier bedtime, more wind-down time |
| **Fighting naps** | Shorten wake window, car/stroller rescue |
| **Multiple night feeds** | Night weaning strategies |
| **Falling asleep only if held** | Gradual transition to crib |

## Nap Transition Guide

### 4→3 Naps (4-5 months)
- Stretch wake windows to ~2 hours
- Cap naps to preserve night sleep
- Adjust bedtime as needed

### 3→2 Naps (7-9 months)
- Signs: Fighting 3rd nap, early wake, long to settle
- Stretch to 2.5-3.5 hour wake windows
- Earlier bedtime during transition

### 2→1 Nap (14-18 months)
- Signs: Fighting one nap, very long to settle, early waking
- Push morning wake window to 5+ hours
- Early bedtime during transition (can be 6 PM!)
- Takes 4-6 weeks to fully adjust

## Emergency Sleep Fixes

**When you're desperate:**

1. **Car nap** - Drive until asleep, park safely
2. **Stroller nap** - Walk until asleep
3. **Contact nap** - Let baby sleep on you
4. **Earlier bedtime** - Even 5:30 PM if needed
5. **Tag team** - Switch with partner/helper
6. **Lower standards** - Survival is success

**Remember:** One "bad" day/night won't ruin everything. Do what you need to do.

## When to Seek Help

### Call Pediatrician If:
- Excessive snoring or breathing pauses
- Baby seems in pain
- Not feeding well
- Fever or illness signs
- No improvement after 2+ weeks of consistent effort
- You're concerned about development

### Signs Parent Needs Support:
- Feeling hopeless or unable to cope
- Intrusive thoughts
- Unable to function during day
- Relationship strain
- Considering unsafe sleep practices out of desperation

**Remember: Asking for help is strength, not weakness.**
